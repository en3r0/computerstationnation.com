I cannot imagine what typing would have been like without a 65% mechanical keyboard. I consider the 65% mechanical keyboard a savior for people like me who can’t get used to its sister version (a 60% mechanical keyboard) no matter how hard we’ve tried.

I agree that they are similar in size and share other similarities but how does one manage to type without the arrow keys? Aside from the ergonomic benefits, there are other enjoyable features of a 65% mechanical keyboard that makes it a fan of gamers and typists alike. I won’t hesitate to tell you that if you are being indecisive about getting one, then you’re missing out.

If you’re hoping to treat yourself to a mechanical keyboard, I seriously urge you to make it a 65% mechanical keyboard and just so you can share my excitement, I picked out 3 best 65% mechanical keyboards that have great features that will definitely add spice to your “key- tapping” experience.

Vortexgear Cypher Mechanical Keyboard

Wired 65% Mechanical Keyboard

See larger image
Additional Images
Buy from Amazon.com
 

Having three customizable pre-programmed layouts that allow you to program up to 100 characters, the vortexgear cypher 65% mechanical keyboard is designed to serve a long typing term. It has a default layer and three programmable layers that can be activated with the combination of a function key and other keys on the keyboard. Each of the three programmable layers has its own LED under the left space bar that indicates the layer that is enabled.

It has cherry MX speed silver mechanical switches that allows up to 50 million keystrokes and a linear actuation point of 1.2 mm. It has no pressure points and functions at a defined force of approximately 45cN without contact feedback. Being a linear switch, it is fast and has high switching frequencies. It has a detachable type C gold plated USB cable and thick PBT Laser Engraved Keycaps. All these features are enclosed in a plastic coated case.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • It uses Silver Cherry MX switches (which is the fastest Cherry MX switch)
  • It has three programmable layers
  • Each of its three layers has a customized LED light indicator underneath the space bar

Cons

  • It does not have a delete key
  • Its case is made of plastic

Epomaker AKKO 3068 65% Mechanical Keyboard 

Wireless 65% Mechanical Keyboard

See larger image
Additional Images
Buy from Amazon.com
 

This keyboard is not just nice to look, it is nicer to work with. It has a small form structure that is compact and portable and weighs just 0.78kg. It employs cherry red mechanical switches that require less force to press down the keys. Having a linear mechanical switch, it has an actuation force of 45cN and an actuation distance of 2mm. It provides no contact feedback and this makes actuation faster, making it a nice choice for gaming and long typing sessions.

It has a keycap puller and features a side lettering technique. It has two shift keys and a multimedia key. It also employs N-key rollover on its dye-subbed PBT keycaps that are made through a process of dye sublimation which ensures a remarkable period of durability, making the fonts on the keycaps last longer without fading away. The keycaps colors are well designed and are nice to tap on, providing a smooth and satisfying feel that helps you type faster and accurately. Being a wireless keyboard, it has Bluetooth connectivity that is very strong and easily compatible. It can pair with up to three devices, allowing them to function perfectly with your keyboard.

It has an inbuilt rechargeable battery of 1800mAh that can last to 120 hours when in use and 250 days when it is on standby mode. This product comes with a one year warranty that covers damages and any fault that might arise within this period. 

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • It can easily pair with three devices
  • It has a keycap puller
  • Its keycaps are made of PBT plastic

Cons

  • Its battery is 1800mAh and might not last very long
  • It is not very sturdy

Drop ALT 65% Mechanical Keyboard

65% RGB Mechanical Keyboard

Additional Images

I am just amazed by the looks as I am in awe of the Drop ALT features. It has a solid aluminum frame and a built-in switch plate. It is fully programmable via QMK which gives you the flexibility to assign macros or custom keybinds to every key. With dual Type C USB connectors, the ALT is outfitted with the latest connections.

A remarkable feature of this keyboard is its hot-swap switch sockets that allow you to swap switches whenever you want. It employs linear silver switches that boast of a 70 million lifetime click rating. For a 65% RGB mechanical keyboard, its RGB backlighting and under-lighting are fully customizable. It can be set to a static color or a combination of custom colors that you like. In order to enhance the backlighting, it features shine-through double shot PBT keycaps and a built-in translucent LED diffuser that operates at a pre-programmed frequency to minimize hotspots found in many other keyboards.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • It is constructed with a solid aluminum frame and a built-in switch plate
  • It has hot-swap switch sockets
  • Its RGB backlighting and under-lighting are fully customizable

Cons 

  • It is prone to double character typing
  • The speed silver switch actuation force is light
