High Dynamic Range (HDR) monitors are celebrated for the unique color technology that it employs in displaying a wide well of realistic colors as well as it’s high contrasts that make darker colors deeper and more pronounced than regular monitors.

HDR monitors are ideal for photography, video, games, and any application that involves heavy image use. Other unique features utilized by HDR technology to provide stunning and realistic images include high contrast, brighter hues, darker shades, and great color depth. Although the degree at which these features are employed depends on the HDR standard that is utilized by the monitor.

Getting a monitor that functions with a High Dynamic Range technology can be tricky, so before you take a shot at it, check out our top 3 picks for the best HDR monitor.

This Monitor employs the best high dynamic range that offers 1000 nits of brightness for a realistic blend of colors and contrast. It has a built-in ambient light sensor that automatically adjusts the brightness of the images to provide a perfect blend of the images with the assistance of its uniquely designed 384 local dimming zones to control its LED backlight for higher contrasts.

The Asus ROG Swift PG27UQ also employs Asus Aura sync lighting technology that provides lighting that can be synchronized with other Aura Sync enabled components and peripherals. This monitor uses quantum dot IPS display technique with 97% DCI P3 color gamut for life-like colors and smoother gradation in the pictures.

This Asus ROG monitor employs a 4k UHD resolution at a refresh rate of 144Hz to provide gamers with well detailed and ultra-high visuals that are smooth and very clean. It also utilizes the unique benefits that are exclusive to Nvidia G-sync to provide crystal clear pictures that are tear-free even when the monitor can not sync up with the content’s frame rate. This feature is highly beneficial to games and fast-paced motion pictures because it minimizes display stutter and input lag to the barest minimum.

The PG27UQ is economically designed with a stand that allows tilt, swivel, pivot, and height adjustments until a comfortable viewing angle is achieved.
