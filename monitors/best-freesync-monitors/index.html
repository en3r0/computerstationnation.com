Best FreeSync Monitors

Computer Station Nation is reader-supported.
When you buy through links on our site, we may earn an affiliate commission. Learn more.

There is no visual distraction that I find more annoying as a screen tear or screen stutter when I’m in the middle of a game or trying to edit a video. An impaired visual display can be very distracting and frustrating to a gamer or a video editor because a smooth viewing experience holds the button to our state of mind at that particular moment.

I found out that an easy and pretty affordable way to help myself is by using a FreeSync monitor and the experience so far has been nothing short of impressive. Using a monitor with AMD FreeSync technology prevents screen tear, screen stuttering, and reduces inputs lag to the barest minimum. It strikes a perfect balance between a monitor’s refresh rate and the content’s frame rate to produce a smooth viewing experience.

FreeSync technology is available in three tiers and these are the FreeSync, FreeSync Premium, and FreeSync Premium Pro. They all function with the same goal although each has some unique features that set it apart from the other. Speaking of others, if you are lost and looking for the best overall monitor page, then head on over there.

FreeSync monitors have a wider availability of compatible displays compared to G-Sync because they do not require a special license to display. This advantage makes FreeSync monitors an affordable option for a smooth, tear-free, and stutter-free visual experience. So, if you want a smooth sail as it concerns your image display, you should keep reading.
