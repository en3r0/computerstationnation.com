Machine learning involves the process of automating a computer to study a large amount of data and make the best decisions based on this analysis, and this is done without human intervention.

ML or Machine Language is best regarded as a subset of AI; the process of machine learning is not an independent one, as you will always come across concepts in the following areas: Data Science, Neural thinking, Data mining, and Deep learning. However, the interesting thing is not just the overlap, but how ML has gained ground.

You will need apps like Tensor flow, Rapid Miner, Keras, and Knime to learn perfectly your trade and master your craft. The mentioned apps require a Strong GPU and this is where a list like this comes in handy.

Conclusion

To succeed at machine learning, you must focus on the task ahead and prepare to make good of every learning process; however, much depends on your computer’s processing power and ability. This is one area where expertise is demanded before a choice is made.

Machine learning concepts are incorporated into medical school, healthcare, and gaming. The list can go on and on, machines, applications, and most software’s built today are programmed to think like humans.

If you are already into ML or an intending one, you already know or need to know that machine learning is CPU intensive. A powerful GPU is needed for concepts such as deep learning and other AI-enhanced processes. You will need just enough ram and storage to make this possible.

The TensorBook stands tall on this list, as one computer you can trust for machine learning, this truth is bettered by the 64GB of ram available on this computer, which makes multitasking easy and provides adequate support for the CPU and the 1TB storage that gives you everything you need on the go.
