]]> Curved monitors are the latest trend that has graced the monitor market, and so far they have earned every right as the preferred choice for games, videos, office work, designing, editing, and general computing.  They enable peripheral vision because the eyes can see the visuals being displayed in the monitor from the same focal distance and this ergonomic benefit is easy on the eyes and can help you work effectively even during long hours.

Curved monitors come in different dimensions, measured in an R rating, and the sharpness of the curve will also determine to a fair extent, how suitable the monitor will be for a particular purpose. R stands for curvature radius, and the smaller the R rating is the more the monitor is curved.

Irrespective of the dimension of the curve, curved monitors are generally comfortable and offer an immersive viewing experience regardless of the viewing angle. So, if you’re considering getting a curved monitor, you should check out our top 3 picks for the best curved monitor.
