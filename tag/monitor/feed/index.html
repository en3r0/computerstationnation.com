For users with low vision a wide screen desktop monitor of about 27” to 30” may be an ideal buy for an excellent resolution capacity. However, several users have contended that this may end up defeating the purpose of the monitor display for the user as it might get outside the user’s field of vision.

Also, some users and professionals have contended that higher resolutions (like the 4K UHD) may lead to smaller image display, which results in image recognition difficulties or the need for a higher enlargement or scaling factor.

Also, visually impaired persons often cannot perceive different resolutions, so a monitor with good interpolation behavior on the low resolution can still be used to get an enlarged display.

It has also been recommended that ZoomText be used to modify the monitor display feature. This brings us to the question of a suitable operating system.

The operating system often determines what display configuration and user-friendly software the computer can support.

The most popular computer OS right now is Microsoft Windows and Apple Mac OS. These two operating systems offer similar features that are suitable for a visually impaired user.

For instance, the visual text cursors and arrow pointers that indicate where a text is to enter and where your mouse pointer is. Also, they carry displays of various collections of tiny pictures, or icons which represent elements you can interact with.

Furthermore, they have built-in accessibility tools that assist the visually impaired in ways such as – making visual elements easier to see and interact with through screen magnification software and describing visual elements aloud through screen reading software.

Visually impaired users who lack vision may opt for more compatible AT softwares that work best with these computers such as a refreshable braille display, Zoom Text, or other touch sensory features. Conclusion

Persons with visual impairment often have specific requirements when it comes to monitoring brightness and contrast, display size, etc. They require monitors spanning a wide range of adjustable brightness, color contrast, etc.

Due to the nature of the user’s needs, monitors should be able to meet at least a certain level of the specific requirements. Monitors that support Windows and/or Mac OS are regarded as most suitable because of their accessibility software features which are oftentimes in-built.
