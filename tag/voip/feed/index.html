The first product on our list takes the concept of QoS or traffic prioritization to a whole new level. The TP-Link AC1750 is one of the best and more affordable routers for VoIP in the market with dual-band compatibility ensuring a cumulative speed of up to 1750mbps. While this is more like a multipurpose router with three external antennas making way for extensive wireless coverage, the advanced bandwidth prioritization technology, in the form of QoS allows you to get excellent VoIP speed and connection quality.

When it comes to the overall coverage, this router extends far and wide up to 2500sqft. Moreover, the strong built-in amplifiers and the high-end, integrated antennas ensure that the coverage is uniform with almost no dead zones to account for. When you are not using the same for VoIP, the router can be used to connect up to 50 devices at once. Not just that, there are four Gigabit Ethernet ports to work with, if you want to minimize the bandwidth loss and make room for faster access. 

However, the most important reason for including this in our list is due to the easy setup or rather device management process. This router offers app-based, remote handling flexibility to the businesses. Not just that, this gadget can also be used for 4K streaming. Last but not least, the advanced security standards, featuring parental control and lag-free guest access further validate the credibility of this appliance.

In case you are worried about the lower cumulative speeds of the router and its relation with slower network traffic management, the TP-Link AC3200 is the perfect appliance to consider. Featuring the innovative tri-band wireless technology, this router ensures that there are no connection drops to account for.

As mentioned, the featured router assumes an equivalent speed of up to 3200Mbps which is more than enough for transmitting VoIP calls. Not just that, this best router for VoIP can also double down as a perfect gaming or 4K streaming router if you prefer. As far as the physical structure is concerned, there are 6 high-throughput antennas to rely on, boasting of Beamforming capabilities.

Besides the mentioned features, this gadget features on the list primarily due to the powerful dual-core CPU that assists with intelligent network prioritization. Most importantly, there are three separate co-processors in play, allowing you to establish simultaneous connections.

In case you are looking for a more simplistic router for VoIP that is capable of connecting 15 gadgets at once while covering something in the range of 1000sqft, the Netgear R6080 is the perfect option to invest in. While this minimalistic device is also loaded with VoIP-compatible features, including dual-band connectivity and overall speed of up to 1000mbps, it is the availability of 4 wired ports that steal the show courtesy of a cumulative speed of 400mbps. 

Most importantly, this router comes equipped with two external antennas that assist in better management of the VoIP traffic. Although network prioritization isn’t an obvious feature, you need not worry about the connection drops because of the reliable dual-band wireless support.

However, this router also comes with guest accessibility and highly secured remote access to better network management. In case you are using it for your home-based office, Netgear offers a host of parental controls to rely on.

Conclusion

While each one of these routers come with a select set of features, the final selection is all about your preferences. In case you are worried about connection drops due to other forms of traffic, the TP-Link AC1750 is the best router to purchase. For budget seekers, Netgear R6080 is the best router for VoIP. 

Last but not least, if you seek raw processing power and tri-band connectivity, thereby eliminating more chances of call drops, the TP-Link AC3200 is the most competent gadget to invest in.
