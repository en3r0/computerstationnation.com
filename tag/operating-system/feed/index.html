Further Info

Settling for the Single Board Computer option may come as an easy choice as most people would likely want the nostalgia of playing retro games. Still, the rigor of setting up a retro console, which most times would require television and not forgetting the high antique cost of these consoles, maybe a little daunting for even the most enthusiastic gamer, hence the need to build a DIY console using the most adaptive hardware one can find.

Many development boards of the SBC variety are regularly used for retro gaming console emulation. However, a laptop and even a desktop often outperform these maker boards regardless of how advanced it may be.

This is because emulators are usually CPU bound and the majority of SBCs are unable to handle the emulation of newer gaming systems like the PlayStation 3, PlayStation 2, Game Cube, and Wii. A lot of these development boards have been known to underperform when dealing with systems like Nintendo 64 and PlayStation Portable ROMs.

Although a GTX 1060, Ryzen 5 powered desktop can easily work on the Wii, PS 2, and sometimes the PS3 emulation a tad gracefully, this makes the Ryzen 5 somewhat of a sensation amongst users.

Regardless, single-board computers are cheaper and smaller hence they provide the best affordable entryway into the fascinating world of emulation. We have compiled a list of our best Single Board computers for Emulation to help you make the right choice for your emulation endeavors.

Conclusion

Using a single board computer for emulation may sometimes appear daunting because of the usual lack of broad-spectrum functionality for different emulators as may be easily accessible using a desktop or laptop.

However, the DIY experience, community support, and affordability of the maker boards make for a very viable option.
