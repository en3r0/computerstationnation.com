]]> As an electrical engineer, or a student studying for a major in electrical engineering, your workload will revolve around; intense programming, while you play around with simulation software.

You will require documentation, and word processing programs, as well as surfing the Internet.

A computer for electrical engineering should house your programming ecosystems like code compose studies and others. Your CAD software, like Eagle and CAD, must also be in the right place to produce the right results.

You will need your simulation programs and software, like Matlab, PSpice, NI Mutisim, Simcenter 3D, and others to give you an edge over the competition in your industry and put you forward, as an engineer who knows the tools of his trade.

All of these programs and software will tax the CPU of your computer system. This brings us to another segment of this introduction; essential features of a good computer for electrical engineering.

A good computer for electrical engineering must have a powerful processor; simulations and programming live on the computer’s processor, same as 2D and 3D modelling, so a good processor is a right place to start.

Furthermore, a good computer must have a dedicated graphics card. There is a difference between the integrated and dedicated card. The dedicated card will reduce the CPU workload and make processing and booting fast and on the go.

Lastly, a good battery, responsive keypad, and the computer’s portable nature will do just great. Our top three picks of the best computer for electrical engineering has all these boxes ticked off, and it will interest you to see just what is on the list.
