]]> Choosing or naming one gaming CPU as the best would be a really difficult task as there are many great options to pick from a lot has happened in the past few years, lots of improvements, new features being added, four cores and eight threads is now the standard even at the low-end.

These days you can get affordable CPUs that offer great single-core gaming performance not only in less intensive games like Fortnite but also in many triple-A computer games like Call of Duty: Black Ops – Cold War and CPU-intensive applications that utilize all cores.

All of this means there are more options than there ever for those looking to create content, stream, play games, and use CPU-intensive applications for a pretty low cost.

The best CPU for gaming is the one that gives that expensive new graphics card you’ve got your eye on a chance to work to its full performance potential, but will also allow you to do more with your machine, Whether that’s streaming or editing gameplay vids, the best CPU will enable you to get a lot done, and we’ve selected a few that still get the job done and give you value for your money.

Zen 3 Architecture CPU For 4k Gaming

Looking at CPUs that are on the high-end, the AMD Ryzen 9 5900X is your best bet. This 12-core, the 24-thread monster is based on AMD’s new 7nm Zen 3 architecture, and it’s said to be the best CPU you can get for gaming on a high-end setup right now.

The AM4 socket means if you were previously team red you may not even need to splash out on a new motherboard, with Zen 3 support being given to last generation B450 and X470 chipsets in the near future via a BIOS update – although you’ll have to ensure your motherboard manufacturer has plans of releasing an update.

Another great feature is, this chip’s unlocked for overclocking, so you can squeeze out even more power if that’s what you want although you’ll want the best CPU cooler rather than the stock cooler if you’re going to overclock.

The best budget CPU is the AMD Ryzen 3 3300X this spot was hitherto reserved for AMD’s Ryzen 5 2600, the predecessor to the current best CPU for gaming: the AMD Ryzen 5 3600. But since the release of AMD’s ‘cheap as chips’ Ryzen 3 3300X, it has steadily gained it’s place on the charts as the go-to processor for gaming budget-wise.

This 3rd-gen Ryzen processor is a four-core, eight-thread machine capable of boosting up to 4.3GHz, all at a low power cost of 65W TDP. Besides all that it supports PCIe 4.0., giving it longevity that beats many of the current best CPUs for gaming. it’s single-core performance is amazing and that’s really all that counts for gaming.

Intel’s Core i5 10600K is a much better value proposition than its predecessor the Core i5 9600K was. The 10600K is a really great chip for the price, as it beats the 3600, 3700X, and often even the 3900X in games. Paired with the best PC case for airflow, it’s also an overclocking monster. If you’re in it purely for gaming and overclocking, the 10600K can’t be beaten.

If you have to choose between the 10600K and the 3600, ask yourself: am I comfortable slapping the best AIO cooler on and know how to overclock my CPU? If the answer to this question is yes the 10600K is probably your best choice for gaming.

Conclusion


With the new range of AMD high-performance CPUs available, recommending an Intel processor as the overall best will be difficult given the fact that you get so much more for your money with an AMD chip right now.

Since AMD moved from being merely an annoyance to Intel, multi-core CPUs have become more common. But even though multi-core CPUs have taken over the gaming market, the difficulty in optimizing games and applications for chips with multiple cores means we’re still not seeing many modern game engines optimizing the powerful CPUs many of us now have in our machines.

At the mainstream end, if you’re really more concerned about gaming and are not so concerned about being able to use intensive multi-core applications, Intel Core i5 10600K is your best bet. If you’ll have a good hand at overclocking, the 10600K looks even better and should be considered more preferably against any other.

If you’re looking for a CPU that will give you great performance and still fit into your budget, the new Ryzen 3 3300X will deliver a great level of hyperthreaded CPU performance, superb single-core performance for gaming, and great longevity thanks to PCIe 4.0 support allowing you to still access the fastest SSDs, all for a budget-friendly cost which is a really good deal.

But with the imminent launch of Intel’s 11th gen chips, bringing with them PCIe 4.0 support for the first time on Intel and improved performance at the high-end, the ten-core, 20-thread Intel Core i9 10900K has lost out to the Ryzen 9 5900X. The Intel chip is more expensive and only really offers a tangible performance difference in CPU-bound games of which there are few.To explore more options, visit Amazon.
