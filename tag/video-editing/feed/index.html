Conclusion

Video editing is highly dependent on having a computer whose speeds are fast, and performance can support both the video editing activity as well as the software required for video editing.

The evolution of operating systems such as Windows 10 and macOS Mojave that require lots of memory demands that your RAM capacity is not substandard.

Having enough RAM is very important for video editing. If the user is editing a video at 1080p, it is essential to use 8 GB RAM at the absolute minimum. For 4K, it better to use at least 16 GB RAM.

The Corsair Vengeance LPX is the best RAM for video editing on the market today although most gamers also prefer it with the memory modules coated with plain, slatted heatsinks made of solid aluminum, and they’re low-profile and the DIMMs are some of the smallest that exist.

It is designed around performance rather than aesthetic features which makes it run at a speed of 3200MHz. The single-threaded bandwidth figure of 15.45GB/sec is one of the best here, and its global latency result of 28.9ns.

The lack of RGB LEDs means there’s more room in the budget to make the memory faster, and the smaller design means it will be easier to install chunky cooling hardware without the memory getting in the way.

This kind of design certainly goes against the current trend for big sticks of memory with plenty of lighting, however, it is better to forego RGB LEDs if it means the memory will be quicker and more reliable which makes it the best option for video editing. For similar products, visit Amazon.com.
