Further Information

AutoCAD, Revit, 3DS Max, and Rhino are 3D modeling software that require almost the same hardware specifications. Still, Rhino and 3DS Max require a more powerful dedicated graphics card (GPU), unlike AutoCAD and Revit, which can run on any number of the decent low-end dedicated graphics card. Whereas design softwares like Sketchup, Photoshop, and InDesign may run just fine on an integrated GPU.

You also need a solid drive for storage that will load up projects, files, models, and software super fast. An 8GB-16GB RAM with an 8th to 10th generation CPU is a good fit for drafting, designing, and rendering at enough speed.

Also, It may be ideal for getting a big-screen computer because it enables the user access to enough space to fit in more tools, interfaces, and bigger design models. Unfortunately, they’re heavy and not very portable, so users might have to opt for smaller ones with good resolution and has enough space for 3D design.

Nevermind that Autodesk sites may recommend you do not use a gaming computer. The truth is users prefer gaming computers. They come highly recommended and for a good reason, i.e., rendering.

The advanced power that comes with gaming applies to rendering images from different programs too. Gaming computers offer less overheating and noise from running high-performance programs. Also, we recommend laptops because they’re more flexible to work with for interior designers.

Conclusion

An interior designer has many options to choose from when it comes to a suitable computer for interior design needs. It could be a laptop, desktop even a tablet.

The best options would be moderately high quality, flexible computers with big enough screen size for design sheets. Hence laptops have made our list rather than other options.

Whatever you do, always go for a high-performance computer or an average one with adjustable capacity features. You can check out some of the best computers on Amazon.
