]]> Most phones now support wireless charging, but it is still faster and more affordable to charge using standard cables. First and foremost, a great charging cable should be durable so that you can get plenty of usage out of it. There are plenty of charging cable options out there. Some are long while others have multiple connectors, allowing you to charge different devices with the same cable.

Micro computer USB cables are the second-most popular charging device for Android and are often used for devices older than 2015. Although they’ve lost ground to the faster USB-C, they remain a popular option for charging phones, cameras, and other devices.

To keep your lineup of devices juiced and ready to go at a moment’s notice, however, it’s good to invest in a variety of charging cables, including the Micro USB. Below, we’re picked our favorite Micro USB cables, each of which features reinforced fibers that won’t split or peel away over time.

Anker says this cable lasts more than 30 times longer than other cables. And since it’s extra long, you can charge your device far away from the outlet. The Anker PowerLine+ II series is durable and versatile, making it our top pick. Some cables, like Anker’s, come with dongles for multiple charger types.

The Anker PowerLine+ II series offers excellent durability and fast charging speeds, making it easily the best standard charging cable out there.

Anker has long made some of the better cables on the market, and the Anker PowerLine+ II series of charging cables are the best out there. Many factors make the Anker PowerLine+ II cables so great. Perhaps the best feature, however, is the fact that they’re extremely durable. The cables are built with braided nylon covering, ensuring that no matter how much you bend and stretch the cables, they won’t break. That’s in stark contrast to normal charging cables.

If there’s any downside to the Anker PowerLine+ II series, it’s the fact that they only support data transfer speeds of USB 2.0, or 480 Megabits per second (Mbps). Still, considering you’re likely buying the cables for charging, and not for data connectivity, that won’t matter to most. They’re also a little expensive compared to many other charging cables, and there’s no USB-C to USB-C model – though there is one in the even more expensive PowerLine+ III series.

Despite the downsides, the vast majority of users love the Anker PowerLine+ II series of cables. Anker are indeed stronger than the average cable and certainly lasted longer than the cables that probably come with your phone. The fact that they’re available in a range of models is a bonus, even if you don’t necessarily need all of those models.

The 10-foot Native Union Night cable makes charging your iPhone super easy when you need a long, strong, stable lightning cable. The Native Union Night Cable offers a long 10-foot design, plus it’s durable and offers a weighted design to help keep your devices safe.

Sometimes you need a little more from your charging cables. Perhaps you want one plugged in near the couch that you can use while watching TV. Or, you want one near your home desk. Whatever the reason, if you want a longer charging cable, it’s worth considering Native Union’s 10-foot charging cables, which are available in a range of different types.

Like the Anker PowerLine+ II cables, Native Union’s cables have braided nylon covering – helping ensure that they don’t break. Conclusion

Before you buy, you’ll want to consider the type of cables that you actually need. If you want to charge your iPhone, you’ll need a Lightning cable. Most Android devices and certain iPad models like the iPad Pro and the latest iPad Air use the USB-C standard. Other devices, like headphones, will use MicroUSB.
