The best 43 inch TV that we’ve tested is the Samsung QN43Q60TAFXZA. It’s an entry-level 4k QLED that performs decently overall. It’s especially well-suited for playing games in dark rooms due to the excellent contrast ratio and black uniformity.

Even though it lacks a local dimming feature, it still produces deep, inky blacks. On top of that, it is well-built with a beautiful design and a user-friendly interface.

Despite being best suited for dark rooms, the Samsung has good reflection handling and still gets bright enough to combat the light in most well-lit rooms.

While it doesn’t have variable refresh rate (VRR) support like higher-end Samsung QLEDs, it’s still a good TV that should satisfy gamers. The TB is limited to a 60Hz refresh rate, but it has an incredibly low input lag and a passable response time. It has a Black Frame Insertion feature to try to reduce blurriness.

SONY XBR43X800H

Flicker-free 43 Inch TV For Gaming

The Sony XBR43X800H is the best 43 inch TV you can use as a PC monitor. It has an IPS panel with impressive viewing angles, so images are accurate at the screen’s sides.

This makes it possible to share content and play co-op games. It handles reflections well and gets bright enough that you shouldn’t need to worry about visibility even in the brightest lighting conditions.

Except for 1440p, it supports the most common resolutions and can display chroma 4:4:4 properly, which is great for text clarity. Input lag is low, although you have to be in ‘Game’ mode to get the lowest latency.

It has a good response time but a 60Hz refresh rate and no VRR support. The backlight is entirely flicker-free, which helps reduce eye strain and eliminate image duplication in fast-moving scenes.

If you don’t have enough space on your desk for dedicated speakers, the built-in ones sound good. They’re well-balanced, although they don’t have enough bass extension to produce a deep, rumbling sound. Its Android TV operating system runs very smoothly. Plus, it has Google Assistant built-in. Although the lack of 1440p support might disappoint some, it’s a great choice to satisfy most people.

Conclusion

Unfortunately, Samsung QN43Q60TAFXZA has poor viewing angles, so the image looks washed out at certain angles. On the upside, HDR content looks okay thanks to its high contrast ratio and wide color gamut, despite not getting quite bright enough to make highlights in HDR pop.

Finally, the unit we tested has impressive out-of-the-box accuracy, so image quality looks good even without calibrating it. All things considered, this is still the best 43 inches smart TV available. You cannot go wrong with this model.
